Forever and a Day

When William Shakespeare died in 1613, he wasn’t too well known outside of literary and dramatic circles. Writing plays wasn’t even considered a serious artistic pursuit at the time. A decade after his death—and 400 years ago today—Shakespeare’s friends published the first collection of his plays. Known as the First Folio, it’s one of the most famous texts in English literature. And it starts with a prescient preface by Ben Johnson, who wrote that his old friend was “not of an age, but for all time!”

Behind the Headlines

A trio of women have been making waves this week, including one of America’s greatest ballet dancers and one of its greatest swimmers.
Tallchief minted
Maria Tallchief, considered one of the greatest ballerinas in U.S. history, is being honored by the U.S. Mint with a quarter featuring her image (pictured below). Tallchief was born on an Osage reservation, and her success in the 1940s broke barriers for Native Americans in the arts. Known for an exquisite technique punctuated by speed, energy, and grace, Tallchief was the muse of choreographer George Balanchine, who she married in 1946.
Nyad’s story
Diana Nyad, the first person to complete the 110-mile swim from Cuba to Florida, is the subject of a new movie in theaters and on Netflix. Nyad, a dramatization of that record-breaking journey, stars Annette Bening as the title character, and Jodie Foster as her friend and coach, Bonnie Stoll.
Barbra spills
My Name Is Barbra, the autobiography of Barbra Streisand, was released yesterday to much acclaim. The nearly 1,000-page book, delayed for years, proved that it was worth the wait, with revelations including romantic overtures from Marlon Brando and Omar Sharif, and nearly casting Elvis Presley to play opposite her in A Star is Born.
